Voter Participation, Open Government and Transparency

During the 2012 Presidential Election Cycle, I worked in a professional political treasury accounting department and was in charge of all campaign contributions for approximately one hundred and fifty clients. This was the beginning of my interest in big money in politics, campaign finance regulations and open government and transparency. I became a Center for Responsive Politics (www.OpenSecrets.org) fanatic after attending their 2-day workshop on using their money in politics data to “find the story.” I wanted to help my favorite nonprofit with their technical challenge so I entered the PDF Liberation Hackathon and earned 2nd place which was a ticket to the Sunlight Foundation’s Transparency Camp and Citizens Advocacy Day in Washington, D.C. That's my picture in D.C. on the left. Placing second at the PDF Liberation Hackathon in January of 2014, was a life changing experience for me and opened up a new world of people and organizations that were in the open government and transparency space. I really appreciated the collaborative, helpful and diverse people associated with the “hacker” community and wanted to become a “coder.” My volunteer work with the League of Women Voters and City of Emeryville has solidified my interest in promoting voting, civic education and participation, and government transparency.
